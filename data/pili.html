Presidio de Lawis (Kota Park): Located in Madridejos, this Spanish-era fortification, established around 1628-1630, is considered the Philippines' oldest-surviving original Spanish military fort structure. It played a crucial role in defending the area against Moro pirates and serves as a testament to the region's colonial history. Watchtowers in Nearby Barangays: Historical records indicate the presence of watchtowers in neighboring barangays like Kaongkod, built during the Spanish period to monitor and warn against pirate invasions. These structures underscore the strategic importance of the area during colonial times.
