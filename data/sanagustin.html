Barangay San Agustin, located in the municipality of Madridejos on Bantayan Island, Cebu, is a coastal community known for its natural beauty and cultural significance.

Sakura Beach Sakura Beach is a picturesque public beach situated in Barangay San Agustin. Originally owned by a Japanese national who intended to develop it into a private resort, the plan was eventually abandoned due to community opposition, and the beach was sold. Despite the change in ownership, the beach retained the name "Sakura Beach." Today, it remains a beloved spot for locals and visitors alike, offering clean waters, scenic views, and a tranquil environment ideal for swimming and picnicking. The beach is freely accessible to the public, making it a popular destination for families and tourists seeking a serene coastal experience.

Immaculate Conception Parish Church: Situated within the Spanish fort near the seashore, this church has roots dating back to the 1600s. Originally a chapel built by Augustinian priests, it became a parish in 1928. The church houses an image of La Virgen Purisima, carved from batikuling wood, which has been an object of devotion for centuries. The church's history is deeply intertwined with the local community's spiritual life.
