Barangay Malbago, situated in the municipality of Madridejos on Bantayan Island, Cebu, is a coastal community known for its natural beauty and emerging tourist attractions.

üåä Temptation Beach (Tagasa)

The primary attraction in Barangay Malbago is Temptation Beach, also known locally as Tagasa. Located in the Riverside area of Malbago, this beach has become a favorite spot for both locals and visitors. With its white sandy shores and clear waters, it offers a picturesque setting ideal for relaxation and photography. Although still undergoing renovations, the beach is already considered "picture perfect" and is open to the public. The best time to visit is between 9 AM and 4 PM, when the sun enhances the natural beauty of the surroundings.

While specific historical landmarks within Barangay Malbago are not extensively documented, the broader municipality of Madridejos holds significant historical sites: Presidio de Lawis (Kota Park): Located in Madridejos, this Spanish-era fortification, established around 1628-1630, is considered the Philippines' oldest-surviving original Spanish military fort structure. It played a crucial role in defending the area against Moro pirates and serves as a testament to the region's colonial history. Watchtowers in Nearby Barangays: Historical records indicate the presence of watchtowers in neighboring barangays like Kaongkod, built during the Spanish period to monitor and warn against pirate invasions. These structures underscore the strategic importance of the area during colonial times.
